# Project Brief: DigitalNest Immersive Portfolio

## 1. Project Overview
DigitalNest is an immersive spatial studio that translates physical architecture into high-fidelity digital narratives. The portfolio website serves as a cinematic gallery of their craft, prioritizing atmosphere, precision, and experiential navigation over traditional layout patterns.

## 2. Core Identity & Design System
**Brand Personality:** Minimal, Architectural, Premium, Playful with restraint.
**Visual Language:**
- **Typography:** Bold Plus Jakarta Sans (Structure) paired with the elegant, italic Instrument Serif (Soul).
- **Color Palette:** 
  - Foundation: Ink Black (#1C1C1A)
  - Base: Chalk White (#F5F4F0)
  - Accent: Forest Green (#2D6A4F) - used sparingly for focus points and "The Nest."
- **Grid:** Editorial layout with generous whitespace to allow "spaces" to breathe.

## 3. Key Interactive Features
The site is defined by its "Spatial Instrument" navigation layer:
- **Viewfinder Cursor:** A custom camera-viewfinder crosshair that tracks the mouse with smoothed interpolation for a cinematic feel.
- **Reactive Atmosphere:** A subtle, cursor-tracking lighting effect on the background that mimics navigating a gallery with a spotlight.
- **Scroll Orchestration:** Intentional, staggered reveals of content blocks to maintain an editorial rhythm.

## 4. Content Strategy
- **Hero:** "Every Space Has a Story." – Establishes the narrative-driven approach.
- **Studio Philosophy:** "We create immersive experiences." – Focuses on the methodology (360 Tours, Photogrammetry).
- **Featured Spaces:** High-fidelity project showcases (e.g., Sky Garden Pavilion, The Obsidian Suite).
- **The DigitalNest Standard:** A "Before & After" drag slider to demonstrate precision and quality.

## 5. Technical Requirements
- **Responsive Architecture:** Fully fluid desktop-first design that adapts the spatial experience for mobile.
- **Motion Performance:** Highly optimized CSS/JS animations for 60fps interaction smoothness.
- **Asset Fidelity:** High-resolution photogrammetry and HDR imaging integration.
